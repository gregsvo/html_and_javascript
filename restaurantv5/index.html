Our Story:

Chef Greg Svoboda decided to dine at an authentic Mexican restaurant in 2012, but found the food and menu frustrating. Having only eaten at Taco Bell, he asked himself, Where is the imitation meat? Where are the ridiculously named items? Why am I not sick when I leave here? With a new vision to answer those questions, Chef Greg took a $800,000 loan from his father and started Tex-Mex Bonanza. Do I have E. coli?

Escherichia coli (E. coli) bacteria normally live in the intestines of people and animals. Most E. coli are harmless and actually are an important part of a healthy human intestinal tract. However, some E. coli are pathogenic, meaning they can cause illness, either diarrhea or illness outside of the intestinal tract. You most likely DID NOT contract E. coli from Greg's Tex-Mex Bonanaza, it's probably your fault.
